summary: >-
  We identify post-selection as the central serial bottleneck of quantum
  automated learning (QAL) and show that projector-mixing QAL updates admit an
  explicit block-encoding with a certified success floor. This enables a
  branch-preserving fixed-point amplitude amplification wrapper that turns long
  training trajectories into a near-deterministic process with predictable
  query cost, and the same certified-floor logic extends to normalized
  imaginary-time protocols and a microcanonical-double lifting regime.
