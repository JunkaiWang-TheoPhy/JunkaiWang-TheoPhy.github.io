### [1] [On higher-order derivative ratios in turbulent flows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21501)
*Zoran Grujić,Muhammad Mohebujjaman*

Main category: math.AP

TL;DR: 对三维泰勒-格林涡（Taylor-Green vortex）中时间区间上的高阶导数比（higher-order derivative ratios）进行数值计算，发现其幂律（power law）关系可结合动态插值-稀疏性（dynamic interpolation-sparseness）机制，通过调和测度最大值原理（harmonic measure maximum principle）为涡度（enstrophy）峰值后的急剧下降提供严格理论解释，表明这些高阶导数比可作为能量耗散率峰值（peak of energy dissipation rate）的合理识别指标。

Motivation: 理解湍流耗散机制，特别是涡度峰值出现后能量耗散率骤降的严格理论成因，以及如何通过高阶导数比识别该关键时刻。

Method: 以三维泰勒-格林涡为基准问题，对时间区间上的高阶导数比（higher-order derivative ratios）进行数值计算，分析其与时间差 \( T^* - t \)（\( T^* \) 为涡度峰值时刻）之间的幂律关系（power law），并引入动态插值-稀疏性（dynamic interpolation-sparseness）框架及调和测度最大值原理（harmonic measure maximum principle）进行理论推导。

Result: 发现高阶导数比与 \( T^* - t \) 的幂律关系形式使得动态插值-稀疏性机制能够产生空间解析性半径（radius of spatial analyticity）的下界，该下界足以克服超层集（super-level sets）稀疏性尺度（scale of sparseness）的上界；由此通过调和测度最大值原理触发湍流耗散机制，为涡度峰值后的骤降提供了严格解释。

Conclusion: 高阶导数比——可视为湍流现象学中经典泰勒尺度（Taylor scale）与克雷奇南尺度（Kraichnan scale）的高阶类比——可能是识别能量耗散率峰值的合理指标（identifier）。

Abstract: A computational study of higher-order derivative ratios on a time interval leading to the enstrophy peak is presented in the case of the 3D Taylor-Green vortex, a benchmark problem in the simulation of turbulent flows. The main finding is that the power law relating the ratios at time $t$ to $T^*-t$ where $T^*$ is the peak enstrophy time is of a form that allows the machinery of dynamic interpolation-sparseness to produce a lower bound on the radius of spatial analyticity sufficient to overcome an upper bound on the scale of sparseness of the super-level sets in view. As a consequence, the mechanism of turbulent dissipation engages via the harmonic measure maximum principle, furnishing a rigorous explanation for the subsequent slump of the enstrophy. This indicates that the higher-order derivative ratios -- which could be viewed as higher-order analogs of the classical Taylor and Kraichnan scales in turbulence phenomenology -- may be reasonable identifiers of the peak of the energy dissipation rate.
